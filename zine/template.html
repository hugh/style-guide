01 — The Problem

Everything Is Too Clean

Body text goes here. Use strong tags to highlight key phrases. Body text renders in the dim color by default, like a slightly faded photocopy.

The Corporate Machine

Subsection content. The world doesn't need another polished SaaS landing page. It needs hand-stapled pamphlets and photocopied manifestos.

✂

Collect

Gather scraps, clippings, overheard conversations, and stolen glances at forbidden texts.

✎

Scrawl

Write in the margins. Cross things out. Write over the crossed-out parts. Layer meaning.

🔥

Print

Feed it through the photocopier at 3am. The worse the quality, the better the aesthetic.

📦

Distribute

Leave copies in coffee shops, record stores, laundromats, and under windshield wipers.

REMEMBER: The medium is the message. A zine handed to someone in person carries more weight than a million impressions on a screen.

03 — Tape & Paste

Featured Clippings

On the futility of pixel-perfect design

Nothing in the physical world is pixel-perfect. Paper tears. Ink bleeds. Photocopiers distort. Staples rust. The beauty of zines is in their imperfection — each copy is unique, each smudge tells a story of the machine that made it.

A note from the editor

We started this zine because we were tired of waiting for permission. You don't need a publisher, a platform, or a following. You need scissors, glue, and access to a photocopier. The rest is just distribution logistics.

The Ransom Note

This is not a product this is a statement of intent against the sanitized web we believe in the power of cut and paste over drag and drop forever
04 — The Rules

Guiding Principles

1

Imperfection Is Intentional

If it looks too clean, you're doing it wrong. Embrace the grain, the misalignment, the ink smudge.

2

Anyone Can Make One

The barrier to entry is zero. Paper, scissors, a photocopier. That's the whole stack.

3

Content Over Form

Say something that matters. The aesthetic serves the message, never the other way around.
