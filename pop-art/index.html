Design System Reference

Pop Art

A bold Silver Age design system inspired by Lichtenstein and Warhol — bright flat colors, large Ben-Day dots, speech bubbles, and thick black outlines for single-page HTML documents.

03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing and thick black separators.

📏

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

⚙️

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 3px solid black border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams and Ben-Day panels can break out to 1100px max-width.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

05 — Pop Art Components

Speech Bubble

A callout styled as a comic speech bubble with a triangular tail, thick black border, and rounded white fill. Perfect for dialogue, quotes, or attributed commentary.

Artist says: Art is what you can get away with. The speech bubble uses a rounded border-radius and a triangle pointer created with CSS borders.

Thought Bubble

Similar to the speech bubble but with a cloud-like border and small circle trail instead of a pointed tail. Use for internal monologue or contemplative asides.

Hmm... Maybe if I use more dots and brighter colors, the masses will finally understand my genius. The thought bubble uses organic border-radius and circular trail elements.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Pop Art system.

1

Bright Yellow, Not Newsprint

The background is flat, saturated Lichtenstein yellow at full intensity. This is gallery-wall pop art, not aged paper.

2

Thick Black Outlines

3px solid borders on everything with sharp 0px border-radius for a graphic, screen-printed feel. This is the most important visual element.

3

Full-Saturation Primaries

Classic pop art palette (green, yellow, red, blue) at maximum saturation. Bold and unapologetic, never muted or pastel.

4

Ben-Day Dots, Not Halftone

Larger, more visible dots than subtle newsprint halftone. These are the iconic Lichtenstein dots at gallery scale.

5

Speech Bubbles as Layout

Speech and thought bubbles are core layout elements, not decorative afterthoughts. This is how pop art communicates.

6

Ironic and Playful

Pop art is self-aware. The design should feel bold, fun, and slightly tongue-in-cheek — Warhol winking at you from the gallery wall.

7

Hard Shadows, Sharp Edges

Flat hard-edged shadows on hover, 0px border-radius everywhere (except speech bubbles). Everything should feel like it was screen-printed.
