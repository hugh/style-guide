03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing against the warm clay background.

📏

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

⚙️

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 1px solid border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Pottery / Ceramics system.

1

Warm Clay Backgrounds

Light backgrounds use warm linen and clay tones, creating an earthy, handmade feel that invites tactile association.

2

Terracotta Accent

The primary accent is a rich terracotta that evokes fired earthenware. It marks headings, callout borders, and principle numbers.

3

Glaze-Inspired Semantics

Four semantic colors map to glaze families: celadon green (finished/stable), raw clay amber (in-progress/shaping), iron red (kiln/fire/alert), cobalt blue (decorative/info).

4

Rounded Organic Shapes

8px border-radius on all cards and panels gives components a softer, more organic feel that matches the handmade ceramic aesthetic.

5

Organic Heading Font

Nunito Sans delivers friendly, rounded headings that feel approachable and handcrafted rather than technical or industrial.

6

Studio Workshop Components

Kiln gauges, glaze recipe cards, pottery marks, and coil dividers bring real ceramics studio tools into the design language.

7

Potter's Wheel Hero

The hero section features concentric circles and a center hub, evoking a potter's wheel as the central creative instrument.
