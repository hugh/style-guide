Mixtape

A warm, analog design system inspired by cassette tapes and boombox culture — handwritten tracklists, tape spool animations, side A/side B dividers, and dial knobs for single-page HTML documents.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Mixtape system.

1

Dark Plastic Shell

Backgrounds use warm brown-tinted charcoals like the molded ABS plastic of a cassette tape, not cold greys or pure blacks.

2

Warm Orange Glow

The primary accent is a warm orange inspired by tape labels, VU meter needles, and boombox LED indicators from the analog era.

3

Analog Audio Semantics

Four semantic colors map to tape culture: warm orange (recording), cream yellow (hi-fi), tape red (stop/record), FM blue (radio/stereo).

4

Handwritten Headings

Permanent Marker gives headings the feel of a mix tape label scrawled by hand with a felt-tip pen, personal and imperfect.

5

Clean Body Text

Inter provides highly readable body text that contrasts with the handwritten headings, like printed liner notes beside a hand-labeled tape.

6

Analog Artifacts

Cassette labels, spinning spools, side A/B dividers, VU meter bars, and boombox knobs bring real-world analog audio equipment into the design.

7

Magnetic Tape Texture

The hero section uses faint horizontal lines evoking the parallel tracks of magnetic tape and a warm central glow like a boombox LED display.
