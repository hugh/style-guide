03 — Layout

Structure

Generous negative space (ma) is essential. Let content breathe. Asymmetry is embraced — not every element needs to be perfectly centered or balanced.

◯

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, 2.5rem padding. Generous margins.

❘

Sections

5rem vertical padding, 1px border-top. Let each section breathe.

05 — Unique Components

Enso Circle

A hand-brushed, intentionally incomplete circle — the Zen symbol of enlightenment, strength, and the acceptance of imperfection. Used as a section marker or contemplative pause.

Emptiness
Ma
Wabi-Sabi

Tokonoma Alcove

A recessed niche — inspired by the tokonoma alcove in traditional Japanese homes where a single scroll or flower arrangement is displayed. Asymmetric padding draws the eye inward.

The Beauty of Impermanence

Nothing lasts, nothing is finished, and nothing is perfect. These are the three marks of existence that wabi-sabi teaches us to embrace rather than resist. In the crack of a tea bowl repaired with gold, we find not damage but beauty.

Kintsugi · The art of golden repair

Ma — Negative Space

The pause between notes gives music its rhythm. The emptiness of a room gives it its purpose. In design, what we leave out matters as much as what we include.

Interval · The space between

Ink Wash Divider

An organic brush-stroke separator — irregular edges, sumi-e ink wash quality. Fades from concentrated ink to dilute wash.

Stone Garden Badges

Organic stone-shaped badges — each one slightly different, like karesansui (dry landscape) garden stones. Five clip-path variants create natural irregularity.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

1

Warm Neutrals

Rice-paper backgrounds with organic warmth — never cold grey or pure white. Every surface feels natural and aged.

2

Generous Emptiness (Ma)

Space is not absence but presence. Let content breathe with ample padding, margins, and line-height.

3

Imperfect Edges

2px border-radius suggests hand-cut paper. Organic clip-paths on badges. Nothing is machine-perfect.

4

Sumi Ink Typography

Cormorant Garamond provides organic elegance in headings. Light weights channel the delicacy of brush calligraphy.

5

Traditional Palette

Colors from Japanese natural dyeing — shibori indigo, temple moss, lacquer gold, torii vermillion.

6

Contemplative Components

Enso circles, tokonoma alcoves, ink-wash dividers, and stone-garden badges bring Zen aesthetics into the interface.
