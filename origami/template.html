Essential Techniques

Every origami model begins with a few fundamental folds. Mastering these creates a foundation for increasingly complex designs.

◆

Valley Fold

The most basic fold — paper folds toward you, creating a valley shape.

★

Mountain Fold

Paper folds away from you — the reverse of a valley fold.

▲

Reverse Fold

Push a fold inside itself along existing crease lines.

●

Sink Fold

Collapse a point downward into the interior of the model.

Folding tip: Always crease firmly with a bone folder or fingernail. Sharp creases make every subsequent fold more precise.

04 — Guidelines

Paper Craft Principles

1

Start with Good Paper

Paper weight and quality determine the crispness of every fold. Standard kami (15cm squares) is ideal for practice.

2

Precision Over Speed

Align edges carefully before creasing. A millimeter off at step 3 becomes a centimeter off at step 20.

3

Follow the Sequence

Each fold builds on the last. Skipping steps creates structural problems that compound.
