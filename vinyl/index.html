06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

1

Rich Black Wax

Backgrounds use warm brown-tinted near-blacks like the surface of a vinyl record, not cold greys or pure #000.

2

Shelf-Light Amber

The primary accent is a warm amber inspired by the incandescent shelf-lighting in a record store that makes everything glow.

3

Record Store Semantics

Four semantic colors map to vinyl culture: shelf amber (discovery), sleeve orange (packaging), label red (branding), cool wax blue (pressing).

4

Bold Condensed Headings

Bebas Neue delivers tall, narrow uppercase headings that echo album title typography on record spines and posters.

5

Clean Body Text

Inter provides crisp readability for body content, like the well-typeset liner notes inside a gatefold sleeve.

6

Vinyl Artifacts

LP sleeves, groove dividers, crate tabs, and liner notes bring the physical experience of digging through a record store into the design.

7

Groove Texture

The hero section features a concentric-circle pattern evoking the micro-grooves on a vinyl record, with warm amber light spilling across the surface.
