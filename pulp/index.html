Design System Reference

Pulp

Yellowed paper, cheap ink, and blood-red duochrome. A gritty design system inspired by Golden Age pulp magazines — woodcut energy, ink splatter borders, and newsstand price stamps for single-page HTML documents.

03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically like pages torn from a pulp magazine and pinned to the wall.

📏

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

⚙️

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 2px solid border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

05 — Pulp Components

Pulp Cover

A card styled like a pulp magazine cover with a bold title, dramatic tagline, issue number, and heavy black double-border frame. Use for feature callouts, article headers, or dramatic section openers.

Vol. XII · No. 7 · October 1938

The Crawling Shadow

“From the depths of the ancient tomb, it came for them all — and no mortal weapon could stop it.”
Thrilling Tales Monthly · 10¢

The outer border is 4px solid --text, with an inner 2px border creating the classic pulp double-frame. Issue metadata and footer use IBM Plex Mono, while the title uses Bitter in --accent color.

Splatter Border

An inline SVG divider with ink splatter and drip effects along a horizontal line. Mimics the messy printing of cheap pulp presses where ink pools and drips at the edges. Use between content sections for gritty thematic separation.

Woodcut Badge

A rectangular badge with heavy borders and a crosshatch-style background pattern created by repeating-linear-gradient. Bold uppercase text evokes hand-carved woodblock printing. Available in default (black), accent (red), capture (green), and refine (gold) variants.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Pulp system.

1

Yellowed, Not White

Backgrounds use warm-tinted aged paper tones, never clean white. This creates the feel of a magazine that has been sitting in a box in someone’s attic for eighty years.

2

Blood-Red Duochrome

The primary accent is a deep crimson red inspired by the limited two-color printing of pulp magazines. Red plus black creates maximum drama on cheap paper.

3

Pulp-Era Semantics

Four semantic colors map to pulp fiction genres: swamp green (adventure), aged gold (mystery), pulp crimson (horror), midnight ink (detective noir).

4

Slab Serif Woodcuts

Bitter delivers heavy, blocky uppercase headings that evoke hand-carved woodblock type used for pulp magazine mastheads and cover titles.

5

Cheap Print Labels

IBM Plex Mono for all metadata gives issue numbers, price stamps, and section labels the mechanical precision of linotype slugs and printing press registration marks.

6

Zero Border-Radius

Every element uses sharp, raw edges with 0px border-radius. Nothing is polished or refined — it’s all rough cuts and hard corners, like pages cut with a guillotine trimmer.

7

Ink Splatter Texture

The hero uses repeating radial gradients to simulate the noise and splatter of cheap ink on bad paper. Splatter borders and heavy 2px strokes reinforce the messy, urgent feel of pulp printing.
