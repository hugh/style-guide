03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically like ocean depth zones, separated by structural borders.

📏

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

🌊

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 1px solid border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace readout label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

Frosted Glass Surfaces

All cards and panels use translucent backgrounds with backdrop-filter: blur() for a jellyfish-membrane frosted glass effect. This creates depth and layering as surfaces overlap the dark abyss beneath.

Organic Radii

Every component uses border-radius: 16px (or smaller variants at 10px and 6px). No sharp corners anywhere — everything is soft and organic, like deep-sea creatures.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Deep Sea system.

1

Abyssal Darkness

Backgrounds descend from deep navy to near-black, creating the sensation of sinking into a lightless trench. No warm tones — everything is cold, deep water.

2

Bioluminescent Accents

The electric blue primary accent mimics the glow of deep-sea organisms. It casts a soft light on interactive elements, text-shadows on headings, and box-shadows on hover states.

3

Frosted Glass Membranes

All cards and panels use rgba backgrounds with backdrop-filter blur, creating translucent jellyfish-membrane surfaces that layer over the dark abyss beneath.

4

Organic Radii

Border-radius of 16px on all components. No sharp corners anywhere. Every shape is soft and rounded like deep-sea organisms — smooth, pressure-adapted, organic.

5

Rounded Headings, Clean Body

Nunito (weight 700-800) provides rounded, fluid-feeling headings. Inter gives body text clean readability. IBM Plex Mono delivers precise instrument readouts.

6

Ocean Semantics

Four semantic colors map to ocean phenomena: soft teal (life/oxygen), pale violet (deep currents), thermal vent red-orange (heat/danger), ocean current blue (pressure/systems).

7

Bathymetric Texture

The hero section uses concentric radial gradients to simulate bathymetric contour lines — topographic depth maps of the ocean floor, with faint bioluminescent ambient glows.
