Spy Dossier

A light, paper-textured design system inspired by classified intelligence files — manila folders, rubber stamps, redacted text blocks, and surveillance photos for single-page HTML documents.

v0.1 · view starter template

Getting Started

Copy template.html and tokens.css into your project. The template includes every component ready to customize. The stylesheet has all variables, resets, and component styles.

03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing and dashed separators on the manila background.

Full-Bleed

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace file-reference label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Dossier system.

1

Manila, Not White

Backgrounds use warm khaki and tan tones like aged manila folders, not sterile white. This creates the feel of a physical intelligence file.

2

Classified Red Stamp

The primary accent is a bold red inspired by rubber stamp ink used on classified documents. Urgent, official, unmistakable.

3

Intelligence Semantics

Four semantic colors map to spy concepts: stamp red (classification), ink amber (annotation), redacted black (censorship), bureau blue (official).

4

Typewriter Typography

Courier Prime gives all body text the feel of documents typed on a government-issue typewriter. Special Elite adds rubber-stamp character to headings.

5

Dashed Borders

Section dividers use dashed lines instead of solid, evoking the perforated edges of tear-off forms and the cut-here lines on classified envelopes.

6

Redaction as Design

Black bars over text serve both as a visual element and a metaphor — not all information is meant to be seen.

7

Paper Texture

The hero section uses faint horizontal fiber lines and a coffee-stain radial gradient, evoking aged paper that has been handled many times.
