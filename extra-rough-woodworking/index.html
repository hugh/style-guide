Level 3 Exclusives

These classes only exist at Level 3. They're the physical artifacts of a messy workshop: sticky notes, tape, coffee rings, torn paper, ink blots.

Sticky Notes

Three color variants: .sticky-yellow, .sticky-pink, .sticky-blue. All in Caveat, heavily rotated, with a torn bottom edge via clip-path.

Pink sticky. Good for warnings or urgent notes.
Blue sticky. For cool, calm afterthoughts.

Coffee Ring

Add .coffee-ring to any element. Variants: .coffee-left, .coffee-bottom, .coffee-sm.

This box has a big coffee ring stain in the upper right. Somebody set their mug down on these notes. As one does.

This one has a smaller ring on the left side.

Taped

Add .taped for a masking tape strip centered on top. .taped-corner puts it at an angle in the corner. .taped-old for yellowed tape.

This element is held in place with a strip of masking tape across the top.

This one has yellowed, aged tape at an angle in the corner.

Paper Clipped

Add .paper-clipped for a silver paper clip shape in the upper right corner, built from CSS borders.

Look at the paper clip shape in the upper right. It's made entirely with CSS pseudo-elements and border-radius. No images needed.

Torn Edge

Add .torn for a jagged bottom edge via clip-path polygon. Good for elements that should look ripped.

The bottom of this element has a torn paper effect. The jagged polygon simulates a ripped edge. You'll want to add padding-bottom: 2rem to compensate for the clipped area.

Ink Blot

Add .ink-blot for a decorative radial-gradient splotch in the upper-left area. Subtle but visible.

There's a faint ink splotch in the upper left of this element. It uses multiple radial gradients to create an organic blob shape.

Dog Ear

Add .dog-ear for a folded corner triangle in the upper right.

The upper-right corner is folded down, like someone dog-eared a page in their notebook.

Splatter

Add .splatter for scattered dots overlay. Like sawdust or wood stain drips.

Faint scattered dots across this element. Meant to simulate sawdust particles or stain drips that have dried onto the page.

Pushpin

Add .pushpin for a red pin at top center. Variants: .pushpin-left, .pushpin-right.

This element is pinned to the board with a red pushpin at the top.

Crossed Out

Wrap content in .crossed-out to dim it and add a diagonal strikethrough line.

This whole section got crossed out. The content is dimmed and a line goes through it. Good for sections that got superseded or abandoned.

06 — design principles

What Makes Level 3 Different

1

Physical Artifacts

Coffee rings, tape strips, pushpins, paper clips, ink blots. Level 3 adds the physical evidence of a notebook that lives in a real workshop.

2

Caveat Everywhere

Caveat is no longer just an accent. It's on section numbers, some headings, table headers, sticky notes, margin notes, the footer. It's becoming the voice of the document.

3

Genuine Crookedness

2–4° rotations on cards, 4–8° on stamps, -1.5° on callouts. Things look genuinely crooked, not just slightly off.

4

Chaotic Spacing

Every section has different top/bottom padding. Borders alternate between solid, dashed, dotted, double, and none. The rhythm is intentionally broken.

5

Destructive Marks

Sticky notes cover content. Coffee stains obscure corners. Tape holds things together. The document shows wear from use.

6

Still Functional

Despite the chaos, everything is still readable. Colors have enough contrast. Components work. The grid holds. It's messy, not broken.

That last principle is the most important one. Messy, not broken.
