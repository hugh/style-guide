Design System Reference

Doodle

A warm sketchbook design system with ink textures, hand-drawn borders, typewriter metadata, and muted ink-tone semantic colors for single-page HTML documents.

v0.1 · view starter template

Pg. 03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing and dashed separators.

📏

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

✂️

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 2px dashed border-top in --border color.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams and sketch panels can break out to 1100px max-width.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a typewriter page label in the format Pg. 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

Pg. 06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Doodle system.

1

Warm Paper, Not White

Backgrounds use cream and parchment tones, creating the feel of a physical sketchbook rather than a digital screen.

2

Hand-Drawn Borders

The asymmetric border-radius creates an organic, sketched feel. Cards also have subtle rotation to reinforce the hand-made quality.

3

Ink-Tone Semantics

Four ink colors (green, violet, red, blue) feel like different pens from an artist's case. Muted and deep, not bright or neon.

4

Typewriter for Metadata

Special Elite gives labels, tags, and section numbers the feel of a vintage typewriter stamp — imperfect and characterful.

5

Physical Textures

Ruled notebook lines, crosshatch patterns, dashed borders, and ink stamps all reference physical art supplies and stationery.

6

No Shadows, No Gloss

Depth comes from layered paper tones and borders. The aesthetic is flat and tactile — ink on paper, not pixels on a screen.
