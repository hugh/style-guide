Design System Reference

Pinball Machine

A neon-drenched design system inspired by arcade pinball machines — glossy black playfields, chrome bumper borders, backglass art panels, tilt warnings, and multi-player score displays for single-page HTML documents.

03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing and chrome-trimmed borders.

💻

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

📌

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 1px solid border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Pinball Machine system.

1

Glossy Black Playfield

Backgrounds use deep near-black tones like the polished surface of a pinball table under glass.

2

Neon Pink Accent

The primary accent is a vivid neon pink inspired by hot neon tubes and playfield insert lights.

3

Arcade Semantics

Four semantic colors map to pinball concepts: neon green (jackpot), hot yellow (bonus/score), neon pink (tilt/danger), electric blue (lock/target).

4

Bold Condensed Type

Russo One delivers the punchy, all-caps energy of pinball backglass art. Inter gives body text clean modern readability.

5

Chrome Bumper Radius

6px border-radius on cards and panels evokes the rounded chrome rails and bumper caps of a real machine.

6

Neon Glow Effects

Box-shadow and text-shadow create the characteristic neon light bloom found on playfield inserts and backglass artwork.

7

Pinball Artifacts

Backglass panels, tilt warnings, score displays, and bumper targets bring the physical arcade machine into the design language.

8

Playfield Lights

Scattered neon-colored dots in the hero section simulate the insert lights embedded in a pinball playfield.
