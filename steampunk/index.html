Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing and brass-riveted separators against the dark leather background.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace telegraph label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Steampunk system.

1

Dark Leather, Not Black

Backgrounds use warm brown-tinted near-blacks like aged leather, not pure #000. This creates the rich depth of a Victorian workshop or captain's cabin.

2

Warm Metallic Accents

The primary accent is polished brass with warm golden glow. All interactive highlights use brass tones rather than cold white or blue.

3

Industrial Semantics

Four semantic colors map to metallic/material concepts: patina green (oxidized copper), polished brass (refined metal), heated copper (forge work), blued steel (precision instruments).

4

Telegraph Readout Labels

IBM Plex Mono gives section labels, tags, and gauge readings the feel of telegraph ticker tape and precision instrument readouts.

5

Victorian Serif Headings

Playfair Display delivers ornate, high-contrast serif headings that echo the typographic style of Victorian-era engineering manuals and broadsheets.

6

Riveted Construction

2px solid borders, corner rivets, and machined 4px border-radius create panels that feel hand-assembled from brass plates and leather backing.

7

Leather Grain Texture

The hero section uses scattered radial gradients in warm brass tones to create a subtle leather grain and workshop atmosphere.
