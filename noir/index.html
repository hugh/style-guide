Case File · Homicide Division · Classified

Noir

High-contrast black and white with a single red accent. A design system inspired by film noir case files — typewriter text, manila evidence tags, redacted documents, crime board string connections, and classified stamps for single-page HTML documents.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Noir system.

1

High Contrast, No Mercy

Deep black backgrounds with harsh white text. No warm tones in the greys — pure, cold, neutral. Like a spotlight cutting through darkness in an interrogation room.

2

Single Red Accent

One color only: blood red (#c82020). Used for stamps, accents, danger, and emphasis. Everything else is black, white, and grey. The red hits like a gunshot.

3

Zero Border-Radius

Every element has sharp, hard corners. No softness, no curves, no comfort. This is a case file, not a greeting card.

4

Typewriter Everything

Courier Prime for all headings and body text creates a unified typewriter aesthetic — like every word was hammered out on a Smith Corona at 2 AM.

5

Manila Tint on Interaction

Cards gain a subtle warm manila tint on hover, like pulling a folder from the filing cabinet into the desk lamp’s cone of light.

6

Case File Semantics

Four muted semantic colors map to investigative roles: evidence green (cleared), manila gold (pending), blood red (dangerous), police blue (authority).

7

Redaction & Classification

Black bars over text and rotated rubber stamps bring the vocabulary of government documents and police reports into the design language.
