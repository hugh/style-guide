03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically like sequential sheets in a drawing set.

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

─

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 1px solid border-top.

↔

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

▭

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace annotation label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

Zero Radius

All components use 0px border-radius for a precise, technical feel. Sharp corners echo the clean edges of drafted lines and cut paper.

A card-style container with a faint floor-plan grid background. The grid shows major divisions (60px) and minor subdivisions (12px), mimicking the modular planning grid used in architectural drawings. Wrap diagrams, spatial layouts, or content that benefits from a grid context.

This content sits over a repeating floor-plan grid. The grid is rendered with CSS background gradients as a ::before pseudo-element, keeping it behind all content.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Architect / Drafting system.

1

Warm Vellum, Not White

Backgrounds use warm, off-white vellum tones reminiscent of tracing paper and drawing sheets. Never pure white — the subtle warmth creates the feel of an architect’s desk.

2

Precise Pen Lines

All borders are thin 1px solid lines with 0px border-radius. Sharp, precise, technical. No rounded corners, no soft edges — every line is deliberate like ink from a technical pen.

3

Redline Markup Accent

The primary accent color is redline red (#c83030), drawn from the architectural tradition of markup and revision annotations on drawings under review.

4

Drafting Grid Texture

The hero section and grid panels use a faint repeating grid of major and minor lines, evoking the planning grids used in floor plans and structural layouts.

5

Three-Font Hierarchy

Archivo provides geometric, confident headings. Inter delivers clean, readable body text. IBM Plex Mono handles all annotations, dimensions, and technical labels with engineering precision.

6

Architectural Vocabulary

Title blocks, section-cut dividers, dimension callouts, and grid overlays bring the spatial language of architectural drawing directly into the document structure.

7

Material Semantics

Four semantic colors map to architectural concepts: site green (landscape, context), material amber (wood, warmth), redline red (markup, review), and blueprint blue (technical, water).
