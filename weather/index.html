Weather Station

A radar-screen meteorology design system — Doppler green accents, barometer gauges, forecast strips, and storm warning callouts for single-page HTML documents.

03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing against the dark radar-screen background.

📏

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

⚙️

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 1px solid border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace readout label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Weather Station system.

1

Radar Screen Dark

Backgrounds use cool blue-tinted near-blacks like a weather radar display at night. The hero features a rotating radar sweep animation.

2

Doppler Gradient Semantics

Semantic colors follow the Doppler radar intensity scale: green (clear/light), yellow (advisory/moderate), red (warning/severe), blue (data/info).

3

Clean Geometric Headings

Outfit delivers clean, geometric sans-serif headings that feel modern and data-driven, like a digital weather dashboard.

4

Monospace Readouts

IBM Plex Mono gives all labels, tags, and measurements the feel of digital weather station instruments and METAR reports.

5

Alert Hierarchy

Storm warnings use bold colored headers that mirror NWS alert levels. The red/yellow distinction maps directly to severe warnings vs. advisories.

6

Forecast Strip Layout

Multi-day forecast panels follow the familiar weather app pattern: day label, icon, high/low, and condition in a horizontal scrollable strip.

7

Isobar Contour Motifs

Smooth sinusoidal SVG lines with fading opacity create atmospheric pressure contour patterns as section separators.
