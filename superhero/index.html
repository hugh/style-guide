Design System Reference

Superhero

A bold comic book design system with thick black outlines, halftone dots, action lines, and classic primary-color semantics for single-page HTML documents.

03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing and thick black separators.

📏

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

⚙️

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 3px solid black border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams and halftone panels can break out to 1100px max-width.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a hand-lettered label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Superhero system.

1

Newsprint, Not White

Backgrounds use warm cream tones like vintage comic book paper, creating the feel of a classic Silver Age issue.

2

Thick Black Outlines

3px solid borders on everything define the comic book panel aesthetic. This is the single most important visual element.

3

Primary-Color Semantics

Classic comic palette (green, yellow, red, blue) with full, dim, and fill variants. Bold and saturated, not muted.

4

Hand-Lettered Labels

Permanent Marker gives section labels, tags, and metadata the feel of classic comic book hand-lettering.

5

Big Bold Headings

Bangers is reserved for h1–h3, delivering maximum comic book impact. Every heading should feel like a splash page title.

6

Halftone Dots

Radial-gradient dot patterns on panels and the hero section reference the mechanical halftone printing of vintage comics.

7

Dynamic Energy

Subtle card rotation, radiating action lines, and POW bursts inject Kirby-era kinetic energy into every page.
