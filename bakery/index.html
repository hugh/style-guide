06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Bakery system.

1

Warm Vanilla, Not Stark White

Backgrounds use warm cream tones like vanilla batter and parchment paper, not cold whites. This creates a cozy, inviting bakery atmosphere.

2

Caramel Accent

The primary accent is a rich caramel/toffee brown inspired by golden-baked crusts and caramelized sugar. Warm and appetizing.

3

Flavor Semantics

Four semantic colors map to bakery flavors: pistachio green (fresh), golden crust (warmth), raspberry pink (sweetness), blueberry blue (richness).

4

Script Headings

Satisfy delivers the feel of handwritten bakery signage and recipe labels — warm, personal, and inviting, like a chalkboard menu.

5

Precise Measurements

IBM Plex Mono for all measurements, temperatures, and timings gives baking data the precision that pastry requires — baking is chemistry.

6

Soft Rounded Corners

A generous 10px border-radius on all components creates the soft, rounded feel of pastry — croissant curves, bread loaves, and piped frosting.

7

Bakery Artifacts

Recipe cards, timer badges, layered cake dividers, and pastry box packaging bring real patisserie equipment and presentation into the design language.
