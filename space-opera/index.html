Space Opera

A deep-space sci-fi design system — holographic cyan accents, nebula glows, HUD frames, and transmission logs for single-page HTML documents.

03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing and subtle separators against the deep space background.

📏

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

⚙️

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 1px solid border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace readout label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

05 — Space Opera Components

HUD Frame

A content panel with corner bracket decorations and a faint grid background, evoking a starship heads-up display. Content sits inside the bracketed area.

◆ Tactical Display Active

The HUD frame uses corner brackets via ::before and ::after pseudo-elements on both the outer container and an inner wrapper. A faint grid background completes the display readout feel.

Transmission Log

A cyan-bordered alert box with a pulsing left border and scanline texture, styled like an incoming ship-to-ship communication. The header shows frequency and timestamp metadata.

Priority Alpha. Long-range sensors have detected an anomalous energy signature in Sector 7-G. All vessels in the vicinity are advised to adjust heading and maintain safe distance. The pulsing border cycles between --accent and --capture-dim over 3 seconds.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Space Opera system.

1

Deep Space, Not Black

Backgrounds use cool blue-tinted near-blacks like deep space, not pure #000. This creates depth reminiscent of the void between stars.

2

Holographic Glow

The primary accent is a vivid holographic cyan with box-shadow glows on interactive elements. It evokes projected HUD displays and energy shields.

3

Space-Opera Semantics

Four semantic colors map to sci-fi concepts: holographic cyan (shields/sensors), nebula magenta (energy/warp), supernova red (weapons/alerts), warp blue (navigation/systems).

4

Starship Readout Labels

Share Tech Mono gives section labels, tags, and metadata the feel of bridge console readouts and ship-to-ship transmission headers.

5

Geometric Display Headings

Orbitron delivers geometric uppercase headings that echo futuristic starship interfaces and space station signage.

6

HUD Aesthetics

Corner brackets, grid backgrounds, and systems status bars bring tactical display interfaces into the design language.

7

Star Field Texture

The hero section uses scattered radial gradients and nebula glows to create a subtle star field and deep-space atmosphere.
