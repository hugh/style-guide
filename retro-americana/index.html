Design System Reference

Retro Americana

A chrome-trimmed design system inspired by 1950s diners, drive-ins, and Route 66 — checkerboard panels, jukebox cards, neon sign callouts, and highway shield badges for single-page HTML documents.

03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically with generous spacing and chrome-trim borders.

📜

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

📌

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 2px solid border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Retro Americana system.

1

Warm Diner Cream

Backgrounds use cream tones like a Formica countertop under fluorescent light, well-worn and welcoming.

2

Diner Red Accent

The primary accent evokes Coca-Cola signage, red vinyl booths, and the tail fins of a '57 Chevy.

3

Three-Tier Text

Signage black (--text), soft ink (--text-dim), and faded chrome (--text-faint) create clear typographic hierarchy.

4

Retro Display Headings

Righteous delivers the rounded, optimistic signage of drive-ins, bowling alleys, and roadside motels.

5

Clean Friendly Body

Outfit provides approachable body text with a slightly rounded, modern feel that complements the retro headings.

6

Americana Semantics

Four semantic colors: seafoam mint (soda fountain), chrome gold (jukebox), hot rod red (danger), navy blue (classic).

7

Roadside Artifacts

Checkerboard panels, jukebox cards, neon signs, and Route 66 badges bring the open road into the design language.

8

Starburst Texture

Radiating conic gradient lines in the hero evoke atomic-age sunburst motifs found on everything from clocks to signage.
