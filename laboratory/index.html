Research Division // Design System Reference

Laboratory Research

Clinical, sterile, precise. A design system inspired by research laboratories — specimen labels, gel electrophoresis bands, petri dish badges, and safety data sheets for single-page HTML documents.

03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically like pages in a research notebook.

📄

Container

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

📌

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 1px solid border-top.

↔️

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace lab notation label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Laboratory system.

1

Clinical White, Not Warm

Backgrounds use cool off-whites and pure whites reminiscent of a sterile lab environment. No warmth, no coziness — clean and clinical.

2

Safety Color Semantics

Four semantic colors map to laboratory safety: bio green (safe/go), chemical amber (caution), hazard red (danger), clinical blue (information/procedural).

3

Thin Precise Borders

All borders are 1px solid in stainless steel grey. Border-radius is 4px — minimal, clinical. No rounded corners, no soft edges.

4

Data in Monospace

IBM Plex Mono for all specimen IDs, chemical formulas, measurements, timestamps, and labels. Every data point is crisp and instantly scannable.

5

Grid Paper Texture

The hero section uses a subtle lab notebook grid overlay — thin blue lines like graph paper, evoking the precision of scientific notation.

6

Safety Data Sheet Tables

Tables use a double-border header with an instrument panel grey background, row hover states, and uppercase headers — styled like material safety data sheets.

7

Specimen-First Design

Specimen cards, petri dish badges, electrophoresis dividers, and safety panels bring the spatial organization of a real research lab into the design system.
