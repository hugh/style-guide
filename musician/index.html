Design System Reference

Musician

A refined design system inspired by classical music scores — manuscript cream paper, staff-line dividers, dynamic markings, rehearsal marks, and fermata pause callouts for single-page HTML documents.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Musician system.

1

Manuscript Cream

Backgrounds use warm aged-paper tones like classical sheet music, creating an environment of timeless refinement.

2

Ink & Parchment

The primary accent is ink black, keeping the palette restrained like printed notation. Color is used sparingly and purposefully.

3

Orchestral Semantics

Four semantic colors map to musical concepts: forte red (emphasis/accents), brass gold (warmth/highlight), rosewood brown (depth/strings), cool blue (clarity/woodwinds).

4

Elegant Serif Headings

Playfair Display delivers the refinement of engraved music title pages. Its italic variant adds expressive warmth to tempo markings.

5

Clean Program Notes

Source Sans 3 provides crisp readability like well-typeset concert program notes and liner notes.

6

Musical Artifacts

Staff dividers, dynamic markings, rehearsal marks, and fermata callouts bring the physical music score into the design language.

7

Staff-Line Texture

Repeating horizontal lines in the hero evoke manuscript paper ruling, grounding the design in its musical roots.
