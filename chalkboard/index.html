Lesson 12
Mon 23 Feb 2026
Objective

Students will understand the process of photosynthesis and identify its key components.

Today we cover the light-dependent reactions and the Calvin cycle. Key points:

  • Light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll in the thylakoid membranes
  • Water molecules are split, releasing oxygen as a byproduct
  • ATP and NADPH are produced and used in the Calvin cycle
  • Carbon dioxide is fixed into glucose in the stroma

Activity: Diagram the light reactions on your worksheet.

Lesson 13
Wed 25 Feb 2026
Objective

Compare and contrast photosynthesis with cellular respiration.

Building on Lesson 12, we examine how the products of photosynthesis fuel cellular respiration, and vice versa. Students will create a Venn diagram comparing the two processes.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

1

Dark Slate-Green Board

Deep chalkboard green backgrounds with slightly warm undertones, like a well-seasoned classroom board.

2

Warm Chalk White

Text is slightly warm and yellow-tinted, like real chalk on a dark board — never pure clinical white.

3

Pastel Chalk Semantics

Green (introduce/go), yellow (highlight/refine), pink (alert/practice), blue (info/review) map to chalk stick colors.

4

Hand-Written Headings

Patrick Hand gives headings a chalk-written, hand-drawn feel distinct from printed text.

5

Dashed Chalk Borders

All borders use dashed styles to simulate hand-drawn chalk lines rather than precise rules.

6

Classroom Components

Chalk boxes, tally counters, eraser dividers, and lesson cards bring the classroom experience into the design.
