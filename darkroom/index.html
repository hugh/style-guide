06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Darkroom system.

1

Warm Charcoal, Not Cold

Backgrounds use warm brown-tinted near-blacks like darkroom walls, not cool-toned or pure black. This creates intimacy and analog warmth.

2

Safelight Red

The primary accent is a deep red inspired by the safelight bulbs used in photographic darkrooms. It provides warmth without destroying the developing image.

3

Chemistry Semantics

Four semantic colors map to photographic chemistry: safelight red (exposure), sepia amber (toning), chemical magenta (filter), developer blue (processing).

4

Handwritten Headings

Caveat delivers the feel of handwritten captions on the back of a Polaroid — personal, immediate, and intimate.

5

EXIF Data Labels

IBM Plex Mono for all metadata gives timestamps, settings, and labels the mechanical precision of camera EXIF data and film edge markings.

6

Analog Artifacts

Polaroid borders, contact sheet grids, film strip dividers, and developer tray progress bars bring real photographic darkroom equipment into the design language.

7

Light Leak Texture

The hero section uses faint diagonal gradients in red and amber, evoking the accidental light leaks that give analog photography its character.
