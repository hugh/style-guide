03 — Layout

Structure

Documents use a single-column layout with a centered container. Sections stack vertically like bulkhead compartments separated by structural borders.

Max-width 900px, centered, with 2rem horizontal padding.

⚙️

Sections

5rem vertical padding. Separated by 1px solid border-top.

Full-Bleed

Diagrams can break out to 1100px max-width with the full-bleed class.

📱

Responsive

Fluid hero type with clamp(). Card grids collapse to 1 column below 640px.

Section Pattern

Every section follows the same structure: a monospace terminal label in the format 01 — Label, followed by an h2 heading, then body content and components.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

These patterns define the visual identity of the Aliens system.

1

Cold Steel, Not Black

Backgrounds use blue-tinted near-blacks like cold ship hull plating, not warm tones. This creates the claustrophobic interior of a deep-space vessel.

2

Acid Blood Glow

The primary accent is a sickly yellow-green evoking xenomorph molecular acid. It casts a toxic glow on interactive elements and highlights.

3

Industrial Semantics

Four semantic colors map to ship operations: acid green (bio-scan), caution amber (warning), emergency red (hostile), cold steel blue (cryogenics).

4

Terminal Monospace

Share Tech Mono for all body text gives the entire document the feel of a Nostromo crew terminal readout. No serif or sans-serif body text.

5

Stencil-Stamped Headings

Black Ops One delivers military stencil headings that echo WEYLAND-YUTANI corporate branding and military equipment markings.

6

Industrial Hardware

Airlock warnings, hazard stripes, bolt corners, and corrugated textures bring real industrial infrastructure into the design language.

7

Corrugated Texture

The hero section uses repeating linear gradients to simulate corrugated metal hull plating and vertical structural ribbing.
