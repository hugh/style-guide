01 — Mission Overview

Research Objectives

The Arctic Research Division operates a network of remote stations spanning the polar ice cap. Our primary mission is the extraction and analysis of deep ice cores for paleoclimate reconstruction.

Station Capabilities

Each station maintains drilling equipment rated for 3,000m depth operations, cryogenic storage for sample preservation, and automated weather monitoring systems.

❄

Ice Core Extraction

Drill and recover continuous ice core samples from glacial formations up to 3km deep.

◊

Isotope Analysis

Measure oxygen-18 ratios and deuterium excess for temperature reconstruction.

▲

Aurora Monitoring

Track geomagnetic storm activity and aurora borealis patterns with all-sky cameras.

●

Data Archival

Compress, validate, and transmit research data to the central polar database.

Safety Protocol: All field operations require minimum two-person teams. Wind chill below -50°C triggers automatic station lockdown procedures.

Core Sample · GRIP-2847

Greenland Ice Core — Deep Section

Retrieved from 2,847m depth at Station Bravo. Annual layer counting confirms continuous record extending 124,000 years before present. Eemian interglacial clearly resolved with elevated CO2 concentrations of 287 ppm.

Core Sample · EPICA-3270

Antarctic Ice Core — Oldest Section

Deepest recovered sample from the East Antarctic plateau. Gas bubbles preserve atmospheric composition from 800,000 years before present. Eight complete glacial-interglacial cycles identified in isotopic record.

06 — Principles

Operating Guidelines

1

Preserve the Record

Ice core samples are irreplaceable archives of Earth's climate history. Every extraction must maximize data recovery while minimizing sample contamination.

2

Safety in Extreme Cold

Arctic operations demand rigorous safety protocols. No solo fieldwork, mandatory check-ins, and equipment rated for -60°C conditions.

3

Continuous Monitoring

Automated sensors maintain unbroken records of temperature, pressure, wind, and aurora activity across all stations.
