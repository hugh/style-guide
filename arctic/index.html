05 — Unique Components

Frost Card

A frosted glass card with backdrop-filter: blur and a crystal pattern overlay. Content appears through translucent ice.

Ice Core Sample · Station Alpha

Permafrost Analysis Report

Core sample retrieved from 2,847m depth reveals atmospheric composition data spanning 800,000 years. Isotope ratios indicate four major glaciation periods with interglacial warming events aligned to Milankovitch cycles.

Aurora Observation · 67.8°N

Northern Lights Activity

Geomagnetic storm conditions producing vivid green and violet aurora displays across the polar region. KP index at 7, with substorm activity expected to intensify over the next 48 hours.

Icicle Divider

An SVG section separator with pointed icicle shapes hanging from a frost line. Gradient from white to transparent for a crystalline, delicate feel.

Snowflake Marker

A geometric snowflake SVG with 6-fold symmetry, centered between thin frost-colored rules. Clean geometric lines, not realistic.

Aurora Badge

Pill-shaped inline badges with a northern lights gradient and soft glow. Variants for different segments of the aurora spectrum.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

1

Frosted Glass Surfaces

Cards and panels use backdrop-filter blur with semi-transparent white backgrounds, creating depth like looking through ice.

2

Glacial Blue Accent

The primary accent evokes deep crevasse blue — the color of compressed, ancient glacial ice.

3

Aurora Color Semantics

Green (observation/capture), gold (analysis/refinement), pink (action/execution), deep blue (archival/batch) mirror the aurora spectrum.

4

Clean Geometric Headings

Outfit provides modern geometric warmth balanced against the cold palette, like a research station in the wilderness.

5

Crystal Patterns

Subtle radial gradients and translucent overlays evoke frost formation, ice crystals, and refracted light.

6

Light Palette, Deep Contrast

Pale blue-white backgrounds with deep arctic navy text provide crisp readability against the icy canvas.
