05 — Storybook Components

Illuminated Drop Cap

A large decorative initial letter, like the illuminated manuscripts of old. The letter floats left with an ornamental double border, gold accent, and corner flourishes (✦). Use class .drop-cap-block on the container and .drop-cap on the initial letter span.

📜 Royal Proclamation
By Decree of the High Council

Let it be known throughout the realm that all typography shall be rendered in Cinzel for headings and Crimson Pro for body text. Any who defy this decree shall face the wrath of inconsistent design.

★ Ancient Wisdom
The Three Rules of Enchantment

First, use CSS custom properties — never hardcode. Second, maintain the four semantic colors. Third, keep the container at 900px. These are the laws of the land.

06 — Key Patterns

Design Principles

1

Warm Parchment Backgrounds

Aged storybook paper tones create a warm, inviting reading experience that feels like a treasured illustrated book.

2

Burnished Gold Accents

Gold is the primary accent color, evoking gilt page edges, treasure hoards, and the luminous pigments of illuminated manuscripts.

3

Jewel-Tone Semantics

Enchanted forest green (growth/nature), treasure gold (refinement/value), poisoned apple red (danger/action), and magic purple (mystery/wisdom).

4

Regal Cinzel Headings

Cinzel provides elegant, regal serifs with fairy-tale gravitas — each heading feels like a chapter title in a treasured volume.

5

Ornamental Details

Leaf ornaments on section borders, double-frame mirror cards, scroll callouts, and crown badges bring storybook illustration into the design.

6

Illuminated Drop Caps

Large decorative initials with double borders and corner flourishes honor the tradition of illuminated manuscripts and fairy-tale storytelling.
